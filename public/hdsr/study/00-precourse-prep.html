Pre-Course Study Guide · Jul 6 – Aug 9, 2026

Goal: arrive at Week 1 (Aug 10) already fluent in the vocabulary and frameworks the course will use, with drafts started so course time goes into refinement.

Topic 1 — Course background & HDSR framing

The intensive is built on Harvard Data Science Review scholarship, so reading source material gives you the faculty’s frame of reference.

Read:

  • ⭐ READ FIRST: Xiao-Li Meng, Navigating the AI Safari: Wonder, Worry, and Winnow (HDSR 8.1 editorial, Jan 2026) — the course lead’s current thinking; the wonder/worry/winnow frame maps onto your Module 1 vision, risk appetite, and prioritization work. https://hdsr.mitpress.mit.edu/pub/m4mz70zp/release/5
  • HDSR Special Issue 5, Future Shock: Grappling With the Generative AI Revolution — the course’s stated foundation. https://hdsr.mitpress.mit.edu/specialissue5
  • HDSR current issue (8.1, Winter 2026) — skim for enterprise/agentic pieces. https://hdsr.mitpress.mit.edu/volume8issue1
  • DAIN Studios, Inside the Harvard Course Where Leaders Learn Agentic AI in Practice (Mar 2026) — direct look at how the course runs. https://dainstudios.com/insights/inside-the-harvard-course-where-leaders-learn-agentic-ai-in-practice/

Exercise: pick 2 articles from Special Issue 5; write a 5-bullet takeaway note in the hub’s Notes tab (tag: prep).

Topic 2 — Agentic AI fundamentals

Executive fluency = knowing the building blocks (LLM + tools + memory + retrieval), the orchestration patterns (prompt chaining, routing, parallelization, orchestrator-workers, evaluator-optimizer), and when NOT to use agents.

Read:

  • Anthropic, Building Effective AI Agents — the canonical patterns primer; the course’s “workflow orchestration” language maps directly onto it. https://www.anthropic.com/research/building-effective-agents
  • Anthropic, Effective Context Engineering for AI Agents — why context management is the real engineering problem. https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/effective-context-engineering-for-ai-agents
  • IBM, What is AI Agent Orchestration? — vendor-neutral vocabulary for multi-agent coordination. https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/ai-agent-orchestration

Key concepts to be able to explain in one sentence each: agent vs. workflow, tool use, MCP (Model Context Protocol), orchestrator-worker pattern, human-in-the-loop checkpoint, evaluator loop, context window/engineering.

Exercise: build one toy agentic workflow end-to-end in any tool (even Cowork/Claude). The point is having felt the failure modes before Month 2.

Topic 3 — Readiness self-assessment (head start on Module 1)

Frameworks converge on similar pillars: strategy, data, governance, talent, operating model/process, infrastructure.

Read:

  • OvalEdge, What Is AI Readiness? Framework, Assessment & Steps — solid pillar-by-pillar walkthrough. https://www.ovaledge.com/blog/what-is-ai-readiness
  • Cabinco, AI Readiness Assessment in 2026 — data/talent/infrastructure/process/governance explained. https://cabinco.com/ai-readiness-assessment/

Exercise: draft 1 page per pillar (data, talent, governance, operating model) for your chosen org context. These become raw material for the Week 1 formal assessment.

Topic 4 — Regulatory landscape scan (light pass; Module 3 goes deep)

  • NIST AI RMF overview page — learn the Govern/Map/Measure/Manage functions. https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework
  • EU AI Act implementation timeline (official service desk) — note: most rules take force Aug 2, 2026 (mid-course!), but high-risk Annex III obligations were deferred to Dec 2027 by the Digital Omnibus. https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/timeline/timeline-implementation-eu-ai-act

Exercise: one paragraph — which risk tier would your prototype use case likely fall under, and which NIST RMF function is weakest in your org today?
