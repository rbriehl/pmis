Module 1 Study Guide · AI Readiness & Strategy · Aug 10 – Aug 28

Deliverable: AI & Agentic Strategy — readiness assessment + prioritized opportunity portfolio + vision statement. Due ~Aug 28.

Topic 1 — Readiness assessment (Week 1)

The formal version of what you drafted in prep. Assess across data, talent, governance, operating model — score honestly; most mid-market orgs land mid-range on first pass, and the gaps ARE the strategy input.

Study:

  • OvalEdge readiness framework (pillar definitions, maturity levels). https://www.ovaledge.com/blog/what-is-ai-readiness
  • Intuz, AI Readiness Assessment 2026: 5-Dimension Enterprise Scorecard — a scoring template to compare against the course’s. https://www.intuz.com/blog/ai-readiness-assessment-guide
  • Skillpanel readiness guide — useful on process/culture dimensions frameworks often skip. https://skillpanel.com/blog/ai-readiness-assessment/

Working questions:

  • Data: what exists, who owns it, quality, access paths, silos? What would the prototype use case actually need?
  • Talent: who are the champions? What’s the skill distribution — builders vs. users vs. skeptics?
  • Governance: what policies exist today? Where does risk appetite sit — and who defines it?
  • Operating model: where does work queue up? Which handoffs are candidates for agentic automation?

Topic 2 — Opportunity mapping & prioritization (Week 2)

Course framework: feasibility × business value × organizational fit. Industry versions add data readiness, risk, sponsorship, time-to-value.

Study:

  • Enterprise AI Executive, 12 AI use case prioritization frameworks — survey of approaches (Google’s value/actionability/feasibility matrix, PwC, C3.ai’s “problem tractability”). https://enterpriseaiexecutive.ai/p/12-ai-use-case-prioritization-frameworks
  • Agility at Scale, AI Use Case Identification and Prioritization — practical scoring weights (value 35–45%, feasibility 25–35%, time-to-value 10–15%, reuse 10–15%, risk 5–10%). https://agility-at-scale.com/ai/strategy/ai-use-case-identification-and-prioritization/
  • Toptal prioritization framework — good on the yes/no pre-filter before scoring. https://www.toptal.com/product-managers/artificial-intelligence/use-case-prioritization-framework

Exercise: re-score your prep-week use-case list with a weighted matrix. Keep the scoring sheet — it goes in the strategy doc appendix and shows rigor in the consultant 1-on-1.

Topic 3 — AI vision statement (Week 2–3)

Course emphasis: beyond efficiency to new value creation. Weak visions say “we will use AI to be more efficient”; strong ones name the capability the org gains and who benefits.

Structure that works: (1) what changes for customers/users, (2) what new capability the org gains, (3) what the org stops doing, (4) a 3-year horizon marker. One paragraph, no jargon.

Exercise: write 3 candidate visions — efficiency-framed, capability-framed, market-framed. Bring all 3 to the AMA and ask which lands.

Topic 4 — Strategy assembly (Week 3)

The document = readiness (where we are) → portfolio (what we’ll do) → vision (why it matters) → risk-appetite alignment (what we won’t do). The “won’t do” section is what distinguishes an executive strategy from a wish list.

Checklist before the consultant 1-on-1:

  • Readiness scores with evidence, not vibes
  • 10+ use cases scored; top 3 with one-page briefs
  • Vision statement (pick one, keep alternates in appendix)
  • Explicit risk-appetite statement and exclusion list
  • Named stakeholders/sponsors per top use case

AMA questions worth bringing

  • “Our readiness gap is X — should strategy lead with fixing it or route around it?”
  • “How do you handle a portfolio where the highest-value use case is also the highest-risk?”
