Module 3 Study Guide · Governance, Ethics & Implementation · Oct 5 – Oct 21

Deliverables: Executive Presentation + Implementation Roadmap and consolidated AI Transformation Playbook. Due ~Oct 21.

Topic 1 — Governance & ethics frameworks (Week 9)

The two anchors are NIST AI RMF (voluntary, US, structure) and the EU AI Act (binding, risk-tiered). ISO 42001 is the certifiable management-system counterpart. All converge on five pillars: transparency, accountability, fairness, human oversight, data protection.

Study:

  • NIST AI RMF hub — Govern/Map/Measure/Manage; know these four functions cold. https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework
  • NIST AI 600-1, Generative AI Profile (updated Apr 2026) — 12 GenAI-specific risk categories with mapped actions; directly citable in your playbook. https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/ai/NIST.AI.600-1.pdf
  • Cloud Security Alliance, Agentic AI RMF Profile — applies RMF to agents specifically; pairs with your Month-2 prototype. https://labs.cloudsecurityalliance.org/agentic/agentic-nist-ai-rmf-profile-v1/
  • EU AI Act official timeline — critical currency: most rules in force Aug 2, 2026; Digital Omnibus (adopted Jun 2026) deferred Annex III high-risk obligations to Dec 2, 2027 and Annex I to Aug 2028. Transparency rules DID take effect Aug 2026. https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/timeline/timeline-implementation-eu-ai-act
  • Covington, EU AI Act Update: Timeline Relief, Targeted Simplification, and New Prohibitions — what the Omnibus changed. https://www.insideglobaltech.com/2026/05/28/eu-ai-act-update-timeline-relief-targeted-simplification-and-new-prohibitions/

Exercise: classify your prototype use case — EU AI Act risk tier, top 3 applicable NIST GenAI risk categories, and the accountability chain (who signs off, who monitors, who kills it).

Topic 2 — Board-level oversight & accountability (Week 9–10)

40% of directors named AI the hardest issue to oversee in 2026 — your presentation should answer the questions boards are actually asking.

Study:

  • Nasdaq, Responsible Board AI Oversight: 10 Questions Boards Are Asking in 2026 — structure your governance section as answers to these. https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/governance/board-ai-oversight
  • ISS-Corporate, AI and Governance: Is 2026 a Tipping Point? — investor-side view of oversight expectations. https://www.iss-corporate.com/resources/reports/artificial-intelligence-and-governance-is-2026-a-tipping-point-for-turning-awareness-into-action/

Governance artifacts to draft: AI system inventory + risk classification, escalation path, human-oversight points, logging/audit requirements, model/vendor review cadence, kill-switch criteria.

Topic 3 — Metrics, ROI & board reporting (Week 10)

2026 is the “accountability era”: boards are fatigued by productivity claims (falling as primary metric, 23.8%→18.0%) and want direct P&L impact (nearly doubled to 21.7%). MIT found ~95% of enterprise AI initiatives fail to show measurable ROI — your credibility comes from baselines and honest payback timelines (disciplined GenAI deployments: 14–28 months, not 7–12).

Study:

  • Gartner, 5 AI Metrics That Actually Prove ROI to Your Board. https://www.gartner.com/en/articles/ai-value-metrics
  • Agility at Scale, ROI and Success Metrics: A CFO’s Framework. https://agility-at-scale.com/ai/strategy/roi-and-success-metrics/
  • Forbes (Jan 2026), 56% of CEOs See Zero ROI From AI — What the 12% Who Profit Do Differently. https://www.forbes.com/sites/guneyyildiz/2026/01/28/56-of-ceos-see-zero-roi-from-ai-heres-what-the-12-who-profit-do-differently/

Framework: baseline 4 dimensions (cycle time, error rate, cost/unit, throughput) → tie ROI projection to the workflow redesign, not the AI deployment → tiered cadence: operational metrics monthly, financial quarterly, strategic (governance maturity) annually.

Topic 4 — Change leadership & phased implementation (Week 10–11)

Reskilling plan (builders/users/overseers, not one-size training), stakeholder alignment map (sponsor, skeptic, blocker — one move each), phased rollout (pilot → controlled expansion → scale, with go/no-go gates tied to your metrics).

Topic 5 — The executive presentation (Week 11)

Storyline that works: stakes → readiness truth → portfolio & vision → proof (prototype demo/clip) → governance answer → roadmap with gates → the ask. Ten slides, ROI narrative woven through, appendix carries the rigor. Use the narrative-coaching 1-on-1 on the ask slide — it’s always the weakest.

Playbook assembly: strategy (M1) + prototype writeup (M2) + governance/roadmap (M3) + one new exec summary. Don’t rewrite — curate.
